Is the Starbucks App a Magic Eraser?

Starbucks Nutrition Calculator


Role: R, App Development

Timeline: January 2023

"Could I get an iced latte with one pump of mocha sauce and two pumps of brown sugar syrup?"

The Motivation

The Starbucks mobile app and website generate nutrition labels only for standard beverages without modifications. For example, an Iced Americano is listed as having 15 calories and 0g of sugar. However, if a customer orders an Iced Americano with 5 pumps of White Mocha, the app will still report 15 calories and 0g of sugar, which is inaccurate.


I hope to address this lack of transparency by developing an interactive Shiny app that allows users to input their Starbucks order and receive an accurate nutrition label reflecting all customizations.


Customers have a right to know what they're really consuming!

The Process

1. Data Collection
The nutritional content of standardized Starbucks recipes was readily available on Kaggle, and I used it as the foundation from which users could add or subtract. However, data on customizations (e.g. syrups) was less accessible, so I gathered it from various online sources. I cross-referenced and validated some of this data with information shared by baristas on platforms like Reddit.


2. Data Wrangling
Fortunately, the data was clean and required minimal processing.


3. App Development
I used reactive values and events in R so that the Shiny app dynamically updates nutritional facts based on the users unique beverage details. Now, adding pump of syrup will properly increase the amount of calories, sugar, etc. in the drink.
